Discover 3 must-visit destinations in Busan

Busan's attractions range from centuries-old Buddhist temples scattered across the city's mountains and coastline to pristine beaches with crystal-clear waters. This coastal city offers plenty to do year-round—families can spend time at a seaside aquarium, shoppers can explore vibrant neighborhoods, and nature lovers can enjoy long hikes to panoramic viewpoints. The Buddhist sanctuaries dotted along Busan’s coast and mountains feature impressive architecture that will captivate photographers.

  1. Haedong Yonggungsa Temple

    "Haedong Yonggungsa Temple is a Buddhist temple located on the far northeastern edge of Busan. Built in 1376, it is one of the few temples in Korea constructed by the sea – you can enjoy views of the East Sea on one side and beautiful mountains on the other."


    Recommended for:
    • History

  2. Beomeo-sa Temple

    "Beomeo-sa Temple is one of the largest sanctuaries in South Korea. It is located at the top of the eastern edge of Mount Geumjeongsa, far from the hustle and bustle of the city. The temple's Daeungjeon Hall is a well-preserved example of architecture from the Joseon Dynasty."


    Recommended for:
    • History

  3. Yongdusan Temple

    "Yongdusan Park, located in the center of Busan, is home to some of the city's most important landmarks. You can enjoy spectacular views from the top of the 120-meter-tall Busan Tower. The park also has 2 museums – check out traditional musical instruments at the Museum of World Folk Instruments and over 80 Korean sailboats at the Exhibition Hall of World Model Boats."


    Recommended for:
    • Couples
    • Families
    • Budget
